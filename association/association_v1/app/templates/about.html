About Us

Welcome to our association! Here you can learn more about who we are, our mission and our team.

Oisis association for sustainable development, which was established on November 17, 2013 in Hadd Al-Sawalem under the name “Al-Alia Association 2”, is an association passionate about environmental
awareness and education on road safety and citizenship. Since its establishment, the association has worked to enhance awareness and sensitization to consolidate the values ​​of citizenship education socially, environmentally,
religiously and health-wise in educational institutions in the cities of Al-Salem, Nador and Zagora,

In 2017, by unanimous consent of the members, the name of the association was changed to “Oisis association for sustainable developmentt.” Despite the name change, the association continued to diversify
its activities and keep pace with school life effectively. The association’s interests also expanded to include royal publishing and making dynastic lands a lever for improving the conditions of rights holders and promoting investment in them,
in accordance with new laws, and to strengthening the autonomy initiative of the Kingdom of Morocco over its southern provinces.

Al Waha Association for Sustainable Development did not stop at the borders of environmental and educational activities, but rather emerged as a supportive force for needy families, especially during difficult
times. In 2020 and 2021, in the face of the challenges of the Corona pandemic, the association made exceptional efforts in the field of social solidarity and health awareness.

On the one hand, the association focused on supporting families in need by distributing food aid and basic materials to ensure the continuity of decent lives for these families in light of the difficult economic
conditions imposed by the pandemic.

On the other hand, the association cooperated with local civil activities to implement comprehensive awareness campaigns about preventing the Corona virus and the importance of adhering to health procedures,
such as wearing masks, social distancing, and sterilization. These campaigns worked to spread the necessary health knowledge and provide psychological and moral support to the community, which contributed to enhancing community awareness
and improving the population’s ability to confront the health crisis.

Thanks to these integrated efforts, Al Waha Association for Sustainable Development has proven its ability to adapt to crises and interact effectively with community needs, making it a role model for social
solidarity and development work.

Oisis association for sustainable development once again demonstrated its spirit of solidarity and humanity alongside civil activities in the Al-Hasani neighborhood, through its support for the victims
of the Al-Haouz earthquake. In the face of this natural disaster.

The association collected and distributed urgent aid, including food, blankets, medicines, and basic supplies, to ensure the necessary needs of the affected families are provided.

The association was distinguished by the integration and solidarity of the efforts of its members and their dedication to achieving its goals. Professor Laila Habch played a pivotal role in
supporting the association, as she provided invaluable financial and moral support, which made the Oisis association for sustainable development a successful model in collective work.
